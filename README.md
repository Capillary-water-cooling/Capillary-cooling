# Capillary-cooling
A new capillary water cooling method for water-cooled PCs and AI data centers.
Presented by **Kouji Murakami** (IPL Intellectual Property Institute)
The biggest drawback of current immersion cooling is poor maintainability, high cost of dielectric fluids, and potential leakage risks. This project proposes a revolutionary **Capillary Water Cooling System** that operates pump-free (or with minimal power) using capillary action, drastically reducing weight and leakage risks.
Additionally, to ensure complete stability in high-output AI data centers, this system integrates advanced electromagnetic countermeasures to absorb back electromotive force and noise.
**Capillary + Master Bag + Copper tube wound heat sink**
Supplies coolant precisely from the Master Bag through capillary tubes to the heat source (CPU/GPU).
Dissipates heat through a highly conductive copper tube heat sink in a closed, high-efficiency cycle.
 **MABEF (Magnet that Absorbs Back Electromotive Force)**
 Magnetically absorbs and suppresses high-density electromagnetic noise and back EMF generated by high-output semiconductors and micro-pumps.
 **MEAC (Magnetic Energy Absorption Chip)**
A micro-level chip integrated into the electronic circuits to prevent malfunctions and drastically improve system reliability.
